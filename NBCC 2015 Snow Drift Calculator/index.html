Snow Drift Load at a Change in Roof Elevation

NBCC 2015 Div. B, Art. 4.1.6.5Cases I, II & IIICl. 4.1.6.7.(1)(a) parapet checkArt. 4.1.6.8 corner drifts

This module calculates the snow drift load that accumulates on a lower roof adjacent to a higher roof, projection or parapet (a "roof step"), following the method set out in NBC Article 4.1.6.5 and explained step-by-step in Commentary G — Snow Loads of the NBC 2015 Structural Commentaries (User's Guide, Part 4 of Division B), paragraphs 36–38 and Sample Calculations 1 & 2. Every coefficient below is calculated explicitly, with the clause/paragraph it comes from, so you can check it against your own copy of the Code.

All internal calculations are performed in SI units (the units used by NBCC 2015). Use the unit switch above to enter data and view results in US/Imperial units — the converted SI values used in the formulas are shown alongside so the calculation can still be checked directly against the Code.

Sources, Assumptions & Roadmap

Where these formulas come from

  • NBC Sentence 4.1.6.13.(1) — specific weight of snow, γ = min(0.43·Ss + 2.2, 4.0) kN/m³. Reproduced in NBC 2015 Structural Commentaries, Commentary G, Sample Calculation 1, Step 1.
  • NBC Sentence 4.1.6.5.(3) — lcs = 2ws − ws²/ls; hp' = clamp(hp − CbSs/γ, 0, lcs/5); F = 0.35β√(γ(lcs−5hp')/Ss) + Cb. From Commentary G, Sample Calculation 1, Steps 2–4.
  • NBC Eq. (2) & (3) — Ca0 = min(βγh/(CbSs), F/Cb), β = 1.0 for Case I and 0.67 for Cases II & III. Commentary G, paragraph 37 and Sample Calculation 1, Step 5.
  • NBC Sentence 4.1.6.5.(2) — drift length xd = 5(CbSs/γ)(Ca0−1). Commentary G, Sample Calculation 1, Step 6.
  • NBC Fig. 4.1.6.5.-A — h' = h − CbCwSs/γ; x = 10h'. Commentary G, Sample Calculation 1, Steps 7–8 (this defines the extent over which Cw=1.0 must be used for adjoining areas).
  • NBC Sentence 4.1.6.5.(1) — Ca(x) = Ca0 − (Ca0−1)·x/xd for 0≤x≤xd, Ca=1 for x>xd. Commentary G, Sample Calculation 1, Step 9.
  • NBC Cl. 4.1.6.7.(1)(a) — lower-roof parapet check Ca0 = min(0.67γhp,lower/(CbSs), 1 + γl0/(7.5CbSs)). Reproduced in Commentary G, Sample Calculation 1, Step 10, and confirmed directly against NBCC 2015, Division B, Sentence 4.1.6.7.(1) (p. 4-20: "Ca0 shall be taken as the lesser of [0.67γh/(CbSs)] and [γl0/(7.5CbSs) + 1]").
  • NBC Sentence 4.1.6.2.(1) — S = Is[Ss(CbCwCsCa) + Sr]. Commentary G, Sample Calculation 1, Step 11.
  • NBC Sentence 4.1.6.8.(1) — Snow Drift at Corners, outside corner: the drift loads on the lower roof against the two faces of an outside corner of an upper roof or obstruction are extended radially around the corner (Fig. 4.1.6.8.-A) and "may be taken as the least severe of the drift loads lying against the two faces of the corner." Source: NBCC 2015, Division B, Article 4.1.6.8, p. 4-20.
  • NBC Sentence 4.1.6.8.(2) — Snow Drift at Corners, inside corner: the drift loads against the two faces of an inside corner of an upper roof or a parapet are calculated for each face (per Sentence 4.1.6.5.(1)) and applied as far as the bisector of the corner angle (Fig. 4.1.6.8.-B). Source: NBCC 2015, Division B, Article 4.1.6.8, p. 4-21.
  • Importance factors Is listed in the dropdown are the standard NBCC 2015 ULS/SLS values by Importance Category (NBC Table 4.1.6.2.–A, referencing Table 4.1.2.1.). Please confirm against your copy of Division B before final design.
How the corner-drift module (Section 4) works: Article 4.1.6.8 does not define its own Ca0/xd formula — it only tells you how to combine and lay out in plan the drift loads already computed per Article 4.1.6.5 for each of the two faces meeting at the corner. This calculator therefore runs the same Section-2 calculation (lcs, hp', F, Ca0, xd) independently for "Face A" and "Face B", then applies Sentence (1) (outside corner — governing face = the one with the lower Ca0, drift swept radially with radius xd) or Sentence (2) (inside corner — each face keeps its own profile, applied out to the bisector of the corner angle). The bisector distance itself is a plan-geometry quantity not given by any formula in the Code — it must be taken from your roof plan.
Known discrepancy in the source document: Commentary G, Sample Calculation 2 (Dorval, gap effect), Step 4, Case I prints F = 1.61. Applying the Step-4 formula exactly as stated (0.35×1×√(3.23×15.75/2.4) + 0.8) gives F = 2.41 — this appears to be an arithmetic typo in the commentary (the "+Cb" term seems to have been omitted from that one printed result; Case II of the same example, F = 2.01, is correct). This calculator implements the formula exactly as defined in Step 4 and is verified against Sample Calculation 1, which is internally consistent. If you spot a different reading of the source, let us know and we'll re-check.

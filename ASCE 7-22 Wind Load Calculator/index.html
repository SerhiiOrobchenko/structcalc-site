Wind Loads on Buildings and Other Structures — ASCE/SEI 7-22, Chapters 26–32

Ch. 26 — Basic ParametersCh. 27 Directional Procedure — MWFRS (all heights)Ch. 28 Envelope Procedure — MWFRS (h ≤ 60 ft)Ch. 29 — Other StructuresCh. 30 — Components & CladdingCh. 32 — Tornado Loads

This module calculates main wind force resisting system (MWFRS) and components & cladding (C&C) design wind pressures for enclosed, partially enclosed, partially open, and open buildings of any height — using the Envelope Procedure (Chapters 28/30 Part 1, low-rise buildings only: h ≤ 60 ft, h not exceeding the least horizontal dimension, per Sec. 28.3.1/30.3.1) or the Directional Procedure (Chapters 27/30 Part 2, all heights) — plus attached canopies, circular tanks/bins/silos, roof overhangs, and parapets (Ch. 30), freestanding signs/chimneys/towers/rooftop equipment/solar arrays (Ch. 29), and tornado loads for Risk Category III/IV buildings in tornado-prone regions (Ch. 32).

Use the Structure category and MWFRS / Components & Cladding switches at the top of the page to choose which procedure to run — only the inputs and results relevant to that procedure are shown.

The calculation chain is: velocity pressure exposure coefficient Kh (Table 26.10-1) → ground elevation factor Ke (Table 26.9-1) → velocity pressure qh (Eq. 26.10-1) → internal pressure coefficient (GCpi) (Table 26.13-1) → external pressure coefficients (GCpf) for MWFRS (Fig. 28.3-1/28.3-2) and (GCp) for C&C (Fig. 30.3-1/30.3-2A) → design pressures p (Eq. 28.3-1 / 30.3-1). Every value is computed explicitly with its clause/equation/table/figure reference.

Basic wind speed, V: read from Figures 26.5-1A–D for the project location and Risk Category using the official ASCE 7 Hazard Tool (ascehazardtool.org), then enter it below.

Items not yet covered by this module are listed in the Roadmap in the footer below.

Sources, Assumptions & Roadmap

Where these formulas come from

  • Sec. 26.5 — Basic wind speed V (3-second gust, Exposure C, Risk-Category-dependent MRI), Figures 26.5-1A–D. Use the ASCE 7 Hazard Tool for your site coordinates and Risk Category.
  • Sec. 26.7 — Exposure Category B/C/D definitions.
  • Table 26.6-1 — Wind Directionality Factor Kd = 0.85 for buildings (MWFRS and C&C).
  • Sec. 26.8, Eq. 26.8-1, Fig. 26.8-1 — Topographic Factor Kzt = (1+K1K2K3)², Kzt = 1.0 on flat terrain. The "Topo feature" input mode computes K1, K2, K3 automatically from hill/ridge height H, half-height distance Lh, and downwind distance x for 2D ridges, 2D escarpments, or 3D axisymmetric hills (Fig. 26.8-1); a "Manual" mode is also available for entering Kzt directly.
  • Table 26.9-1, Note 2 — Ground Elevation Factor Ke = exp(-0.0000362·ze), ze in ft (Ke=1.0 always permitted per Note 1).
  • Table 26.10-1, Note 1 & footnote * — Velocity pressure exposure coefficient Kh: Kh=2.41(15/zg)2/α for h<15 ft, Kh=2.41(h/zg)2/α for 15≤h≤zg, Kh=2.41 for h>zg; α/zg from Table 26.11-1 (Exposure B: α=7.5, zg=3280 ft; C: α=9.8, zg=2460 ft; D: α=11.5, zg=1935 ft). Footnote *: for Exposure B and h<30 ft, use Kh=0.70 in Chapter 28.
  • Eq. 26.10-1 — qh = 0.00256·Kh·Kzt·Ke·V² (psf, V in mph).
  • Sec. 26.2, Table 26.13-1 — Enclosure classification (Enclosed/Partially Enclosed/Partially Open/Open, per Sec. 26.2 definitions) and Internal Pressure Coefficient (GCpi): Open = 0.00; Partially Open & Enclosed = ±0.18; Partially Enclosed = ±0.55. Both signs must be checked (Table 26.13-1, Note 1).
  • Sec. 27.3.1, Eq. 27.3-1 — MWFRS Directional Procedure, applicable to enclosed/partially enclosed buildings of any height: p = qGCp−qi(GCpi), using qz (varying with height per Table 26.10-1) on the windward wall and qh elsewhere. Wall Cp: windward +0.8 (all L/B); leeward −0.5→−0.2 by L/B (Fig. 27.3-1, linear interpolation for 1≤L/B≤4); side walls −0.7 (constant). Roof Cp (wind normal to ridge) is read from Fig. 27.3-1 as a function of θ and h/L for flat/low-slope roofs (θ≤10°, two Cp values per cell per Note 3, both checked) and for sloped roofs (θ>10°). Sec. 27.3.5 roof-overhang soffit Cp=+0.8 (no (GCpi) term) is also implemented within this procedure.
  • Eq. 28.3-1 — MWFRS design pressure: p = qhKd[(GCpf)−(GCpi)]. Applicable to enclosed/partially enclosed buildings, h≤60 ft, per Sec. 28.3.1.
  • Figure 28.3-1, Notation — Zone dimension a = min[0.1×(least horizontal dimension), 0.4h], not less than max[0.04×(least horizontal dimension), 3 ft]; capped at 0.8h for θ=0–7° with least horizontal dimension >300 ft.
  • Figure 28.3-1 — (GCpf) for Load Cases 1 (Zones 1–4, 1E–4E, θ-dependent at θ=0–5°, 20°, 30–45°, 90°, linear interpolation permitted between) and 2 (Zones 1–6, 1E–6E, all θ), per Sec. 28.3.2.1, evaluated with each corner as the windward corner.
  • Figure 28.3-2, Sec. 28.3.2 — Torsional Load Cases 3 (LC1 zones + T-zones 1T–4T) and 4 (LC2 zones + T-zones 5T,6T), required when h>30 ft unless a Sec. 28.3.2 exception applies. T-zone (GCpf) values are θ-dependent for 1T–4T and constant for 5T/6T.
  • Eq. 30.3-1 — C&C design pressure: p = qhKd[(GCp)−(GCpi)]. Applicable to enclosed/partially enclosed low-rise buildings per Sec. 30.3.1.
  • Figure 30.3-1 — Wall C&C (GCp), Zones 4 & 5, tabulated at effective wind area A≤10 ft² and A≥500 ft², log-linear interpolation between (Zone 4: −1.1→−0.8; Zone 5: −1.4→−0.8; positive both zones: +1.0→+0.7). Applies for all θ.

  • For each zone, pmin (outward/suction) and pmax (inward/positive) pair the (GCpf)/(GCp) value with whichever sign of (GCpi) produces the larger-magnitude net pressure — both signs of (GCpi) must be evaluated per Table 26.13-1, Note 1.
  • Sec. 30.7 — Roof overhangs: net (GCp) = roof-surface (GCp) (top of overhang, Fig. 30.3-2A/2B/2C/2D–G equivalent per θ/roof shape) − wall-zone (GCp) (bottom/soffit, Fig. 30.3-1), both at the roof effective wind area; roof Zone 2↔wall Zone 4 and roof Zone 3↔wall Zone 5. No (GCpi) term (both overhang faces are exterior). Combination approach, worked example, and sign convention per "ASCE 7-22 Changes to Component and Cladding Wind Provisions" (StructureMag, 2022) and the Eng-Tips thread "ASCE 7-22 Roof overhang pressure" (Nov 2025); the roof↔wall zone pairing is this calculator's engineering judgment — see the "i" button for details and verify against Sec. 30.7/Fig. 30.7-1 for critical designs.
  • Sec. 27.3.4, Eq. 27.3-3 — MWFRS parapet pressure pp=qpKd(GCpn), (GCpn)=+1.5 (windward) / −1.0 (leeward), qp evaluated at the top of the parapet using the Table 26.10-1 Kh formula at z=h+parapet height. (GCpn) values corroborated by RISA "Load Generation — Wind Loads" documentation and the ICC "Demystifying Loads for Building Officials" ASCE 7-22 guide. Total combined pressure = windward pp − leeward pp (sum of magnitudes). This calculator's Envelope Procedure (Ch. 28) parapet provision is cited provisionally as Sec. 28.3.4, by analogy to the ASCE 7-16 Sec. 27.3.4↔28.3.2 correspondence (Meca Enterprises); this section number is unverified for ASCE 7-22 — see "i" button.
  • Sec. 30.6, Eq. 30.6-1 — C&C parapet pressure p=qp[(GCp)−(GCpi)], (GCpi)=0 (both faces exterior, as with overhangs). Two load cases per ASCE 7-22 Sec. 30.6 (Load Case A / Load Case B), consistent with Meca Enterprises' "Wind Load on Parapets" article: Load A = wall (GCp)pos (Fig. 30.3-1, Zone 4 or 5) − paired roof (GCp)neg (Fig. 30.3-2A–G, Zone 2 or 3); Load B = wall (GCp)pos − wall (GCp)neg (same zone). Wall Zone 4↔roof Zone 2 and wall Zone 5↔roof Zone 3 pairing is this calculator's engineering judgment (inverse of the Sec. 30.7 overhang pairing) — numerically consistent with Meca's example (wall Zone 4 (GCp)pos=+1.0, roof Zone 2 (GCp)neg=−2.3 at A=10 ft², matching this calculator's tables). Not implemented: Fig. 30.3-1 Note 5 reduction and the Fig. 30.3-2A Zone 3=Zone 2 substitution for parapets ≥3 ft — verify against the Standard directly. See "i" button for details.
  • Ch. 29 — Other Structures: F = qhKdGCfAs (Eq. 29.3-1, Fig. 29.3-1) for solid freestanding signs/walls (Sec. 29.3); F = qzKdGCfAf (Eq. 29.4-1) for chimneys/tanks (Fig. 29.4-1), open signs/single-plane frames (Fig. 29.4-2), and trussed towers (Fig. 29.4-3) (Sec. 29.4); Fh/Fv = qhKd(GCr)Af/Ar (Eqs. 29.4-2/3) for rooftop structures/equipment, with derived C&C tributary pressures pwall = Fh/Awall, proof = Fv/Aroof per Sec. 30.8; p = qhKd(GCrn), (GCrn)=γpγcγE(GCrn)nom (Eqs. 29.4-5/6) for rooftop solar panels (Sec. 29.4.3); Fn/Mc = qhKd(GCgn)A / qhKd(GCgm)ALc (Eqs. 29.4-8/9) for ground-mounted fixed-tilt solar panel systems (Sec. 29.4.5), with (GCgn)/(GCgm) = static term (Fig. 29.4-10) + dynamic term (Fig. 29.4-11, Eq. 29.4-12, Ns=0.682·n·Lc/V, scaled for damping per Note 1), both digitized directly from the figures and combined per Eqs. 29.4-10/29.4-11.
  • Ch. 32 — Tornado Loads, Secs. 32.1–32.17: applies to Risk Category III/IV buildings in tornado-prone regions where the tornado speed VT ≥ 60 mph and VT ≥ a threshold × V (Sec. 32.1.1). VT is read from Figs. 32.5-1 (RC III) or 32.5-2 (RC IV) via the ASCE Hazard Tool, selected by effective plan area Ae (Sec. 32.5.1/32.5.4). C&C directionality factor KdT = 0.75 (standard) or 1.0 for essential facilities (Table 32.6-1).
  • Sec. 27.3.2, Eq. 27.3-2 — "Open Buildings with Monoslope, Pitched, or Troughed Free Roofs": net design pressure p = qhKdGCN, applied normal to the roof surface. G = 0.85 for rigid structures per Sec. 26.11.1 (the flexible-structure Gf procedure, Secs. 26.11.3–26.11.5, is not implemented). CN (net pressure coefficient, Load Cases A & B) is read from Figs. 27.3-4 (Monoslope, γ=0°/180°, 0.25≤h/L≤1.0, θ≤45°), 27.3-5 (Pitched, γ=0°/180°), 27.3-6 (Troughed, γ=0°/180°, the sign-flip of Fig. 27.3-5), and 27.3-7 (γ=90°/270°, all shapes/h/L, 3 zones by distance from the windward edge), with linear interpolation between tabulated θ rows (7.5°–45°) per Fig. 27.3-4, Note 3. Clear vs. obstructed wind flow (Note 2) selects which CN column is used. (GCpi)=0 for this procedure (no enclosed volume). Per Fig. 27.3-4, Note 4, for 0.05≤h/L<0.25 and θ<5° (monoslope), Fig. 27.3-7 governs the γ=0°/180° case instead of Fig. 27.3-4 — implemented and flagged. Applicability: this module implements the Envelope Procedure (Chapters 28 & 30, Part 1) for enclosed or partially enclosed buildings with a flat, gable, or hip roof, mean roof height h ≤ 60 ft, and h not exceeding the least horizontal dimension (Sec. 28.3.1 / 30.3.1 conditions). Open and partially open buildings with Kzt>1 inputs are computed arithmetically but the underlying figures (28.3-1/30.3-1/30.3-2A) are derived for enclosed/partially enclosed buildings — verify applicability for your specific case.

Not yet included

  • Roof pressures for isolated and grouped circular bins/tanks (Secs. 30.10.4 & 30.10.6) — not implemented; these provisions exist only as graphical figures in ASCE/SEI 7-22, with no numeric (GCp) values recoverable from the Standard's text or Commentary.
  • Flexible / dynamically sensitive structures — the Gf gust-effect factor procedure (Secs. 26.11.3–26.11.5, requires the fundamental natural frequency) is not implemented; this calculator uses G = 0.85 for rigid structures throughout (Sec. 26.11.1).
  • Two parapet C&C refinements: the Fig. 30.3-1 Note 5 wall (GCp) reduction, and the Fig. 30.3-2A note permitting roof Zone 3 (GCp) = Zone 2 (GCp) for parapets ≥ 3 ft — not implemented; see the parapet "i" button for details.
  • Stepped roofs (Fig. 30.3-3), multispan gable roofs (Fig. 30.3-4, all θ, including θ > 10° via digitized graph breakpoints), monoslope roofs (Fig. 30.3-5A/5B, 3° < θ ≤ 30°, with zone geometry confirmed from the user's own CAD-traced diagrams), sawtooth roofs (Fig. 30.3-6, all θ, including θ > 10° via digitized graph breakpoints), and domed roofs (Fig. 30.3-7) are all implemented above. Monoslope roofs at θ ≤ 3° or θ > 30° (outside both figures' plotted range) and domed roofs outside Fig. 30.3-7 Note 4's range (0 ≤ hD/D ≤ 0.5, 0.2 ≤ f/D ≤ 0.5) are flagged rather than extrapolated, since neither has a stated alternative in the Standard's text.
  • Ground-mounted solar panel zone geometry (Fig. 29.4-9, interior vs. perimeter) is user-selected directly rather than computed from array layout, since no diagram/CAD source was available for that figure (unlike the monoslope-roof case, where exact DXF diagrams were supplied). Both the static (Fig. 29.4-10) and dynamic (Fig. 29.4-11) gust coefficient terms for this structure type are implemented.
  • Chapter 31 Wind Tunnel Procedure — out of scope for a code-formula calculator; requires physical or CFD testing.
  • Automatic lookup of basic wind speed V from the ASCE Hazard Tool (currently entered manually, as with pg in the Snow module). The topographic factor Kzt (Fig. 26.8-1), by contrast, now has an automatic computation option (2D ridge/escarpment or 3D hill) alongside manual entry.
